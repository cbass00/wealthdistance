It can be incredibly difficult for the average person to imagine... really imagine the amount of money at the disposal of the ultra-rich.

The human brain is terrible at understanding big numbers.

Sometimes changing the unit of measurement can help make sense of things.

For example...

The median household income in the United States is $68,000 per year.

68,000 feet = 12.8 miles

I'll use my hometown as a reference.

That's about the distance between Bailey, NC and Wilson, NC.

It takes about 17 minutes to make that trip, going an average of 45mph.

1 million dollars sounds like a lot of money...

It's a life-changing amount of money to most people.

1,000,000 feet = 189 miles

That's the distance between Bailey, NC and Charlotte, NC.

This trip would take about 3 hours going an average of 60mph...

It would take 4 hours going an average of 45mph.

But what about even bigger amounts of money? Like 1 billion dollars?

If you had to guess, how far do you think 1 billion dollars would take you?

  • From the US to China?
  • Around the world?
  • To the moon?

The distance around the entire Earth is 24,902 miles.

1 billion feet = 189,394 miles

You could travel from New York City to Beijing 17 times.

You could circle the entire Earth 7.6 times.

Or you could make it 80% of the way to the moon.

At 45mph, it would take you 4,208 hours, or 25 weeks.

Jeff Bezos is currently the richest man in the US.

His wealth is estimated to be around 185 billion dollars in 2021.

185 billion feet = 35,037,879 miles

That's like traveling from New York City to Beijing 3,186 times.

Or circling the Earth 1,400 times

There is only one Jeff Bezos...

But there are 614 billionaires in the US.

9% of households in the US make less than $15,000 a year.

Those households could go 2.8 miles in our money-to-distance analogy.

At 45mph, it would take them 3 minutes and 45 seconds.

The federal minimum wage in 2021 is $7.25/hour.

A minimum wage worker, working 40 hours a week, makes about $15,000 a year.

Disabled people in America receive an average of $1,280/month in benefits as of 2021.

That's $15,680/year.

The median lifetime earnings of an American worker comes in at 1.7 million dollars,

or an average of $42,000 a year, at $20/hour.

That's 322 miles in 7 hours.

There's such a vast gulf between 2.8 miles and 35 million miles that it's hard to visualize.

Imagine 3 minutes and 45 seconds compared to 89 years.

That's higher than the life expectancy in the United States.

There is no job or career that exists that enables a single human to "earn" that kind of money.

Money like this isn't earned - it's stolen via exploitation.
